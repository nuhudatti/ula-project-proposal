üß± PART 2: Load Image and Extract Text

gray = cv2.cvtColor(img, cv2.COLOR_BGR2GRAY)
raw_text = pytesseract.image_to_string(gray)
lines = raw_text.split('\n')
lines = [line.strip() for line in lines if line.strip() != '']

print("Cleaned Lines:")
for line in lines:
    print(line)

‚úÖ Explanation: Reads your image and cleans the text line-by-line.

üß± PART 3: Extract Items and Prices

Paste this after Part 2:

expenses = []

for line in lines:
    parts = line.rsplit('‚Ç¶', 1)
    if len(parts) == 2:
        item = parts[0].strip()
        price_str = parts[1].replace(',', '').strip()
        try:
            price = int(price_str)
            expenses.append({'item': item, 'price': price})
        except ValueError:
            pass

‚úÖ Explanation: This turns lines into structured data (dictionary format).

üß± PART 4 (Optional): Show and Save Results

print("\nStructured Data:")
for e in expenses:
    print(f"{e['item']} - ‚Ç¶{e['price']}")

with open('expenses.csv', 'w', newline='') as file:
    writer = csv.writer(file)
    writer.writerow(['Item', 'Price'])
    for e in expenses:
        writer.writerow([e['item'], e['price']])

‚úÖ Explanation: Prints the results and saves a file expenses.csv
